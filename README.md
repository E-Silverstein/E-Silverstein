## Welcome - I’m Ethan

I focus on building intelligent systems that are dependable, rigorously engineered, and capable of operating in complex environments. I’m drawn to challenging technical problems—especially those involving autonomy, learning under uncertainty, constrained compute, and high-stakes deployment conditions.

My work blends machine learning, autonomy, systems engineering, and high-performance computing, with an emphasis on UAVs, resilient communications, and decision-support systems that must perform reliably when conditions are unpredictable or degraded.

I am currently completing my accelerated MS/BS in Computer Science at Stevens Institute of Technology, where I split my time between two research environments that shape how I approach hard technical problems.

---

### Research Foundations

#### Mobile AI Cybersecurity & Computing (MACC) Lab

At the MACC Lab, I work on applying machine learning to demanding, non-ideal, and resource-constrained scenarios. The systems we build are intended for environments where data is imperfect, compute is limited, networks are unreliable, and the cost of a model behaving unpredictably is high.

My experience includes UAV anomaly detection, ML-based 5G NTN research for LEO satellite communications, and ORAN xApps that enable intelligent UAV communication. The focus is on delivering solutions that hold up under real-world pressure: efficient, explainable, robust, and capable of meeting strict latency and performance requirements.

#### Systems Engineering Research Center (SERC)

SERC pushed me to think beyond algorithms and code—to consider architecture, integration, testing, lifecycle, and the human element that determines whether technology is usable and trustworthy.

This work reinforced a system-level mindset: solving hard problems requires more than a strong model. It demands the coordination of software, hardware, operators, safety mechanisms, validation pathways, and maintainability. Building AI that matters means designing for long-term reliability and real operational constraints.

### Air Force Research Laboratory Experience

I completed two research internships at the Air Force Research Laboratory focused on applying AI to defense challenges that do not have clean boundaries or straightforward solutions. I worked on distributed command and control, multi-agent decision-making, and logistics optimization—domains that require reasoning under uncertainty and rapidly changing conditions.

This involved designing custom Dec-POMDP environments, training multi-agent reinforcement learning policies, and evaluating whether solutions would meaningfully support real operators. The experience sharpened my interest in difficult, mission-driven technical problems that require discipline, creativity, and a deep respect for reliability.

### Focus Areas

Much of my work centers around the following areas:

#### AI for Complex Decision-Making

Developing models that reason under uncertainty, detect and adapt to non-standard behavior, and support decision-making in fast-changing or incomplete information environments. This includes anomaly detection, multi-agent RL, adaptive planning, and learning-based control.

#### Autonomy and Distributed Systems

Investigating how multiple agents coordinate, exchange information, and act coherently without centralized control. Examples include UAV swarms, distributed RL, and edge-based intelligence via xApps for time-sensitive decisions.

#### Real-Time and Efficient ML Deployment

Turning research into software engineered for technical adversity. This includes model optimization (PyTorch to ONNX/TensorRT), GPU/CUDA acceleration, real-time inference APIs, and techniques that preserve performance under strict latency, compute, and memory limits.

#### Systems Engineering of AI-Enabled Capabilities

Ensuring that AI functions as part of a reliable, maintainable, and testable system. This includes architecture, validation, operational safety, human-machine interaction, integration across components, and lifecycle sustainment.
